Dataset Information

This analysis examines marine species counts and environmental variables from the SHR_2022-2023_fauna_ctd_calcO2_pressure.csv dataset. The dataset contains 50,841 observations with the following variables:

Species Variables:

  • Sablefish
  • Sea Stars
  • Crabs
  • Hagfish
  • Euphausia
  • Liponema
  • Flatfish
  • Rockfish
  • Eelpout

Environmental Variables:

  • Temperature
  • Conductivity
  • Pressure
  • Salinity
  • Oxygen (ml/l)
  • PressurePSI

Temporal Variables:

  • Timestamp
  • Date
  • Time

Substrate Variables:

  • Biogenic Substrate
  • Non-Biogenic Substrate

1. Generalized Additive Models (GAMs)

Purpose: Capture nonlinear relationships between environmental predictors and species counts over time.

Key Findings: GAMs revealed complex nonlinear relationships between environmental variables and species abundance. Rockfish showed the strongest relationship with environmental predictors (R² = 0.0807), suggesting their distribution is more predictable based on environmental conditions.

2. Generalized Linear Mixed Models (GLMMs)

Purpose: Handle count data while accounting for random effects and autocorrelation.

Key Findings: GLMMs were used to model species presence/absence with time periods as random effects. The models identified significant environmental predictors for each species while accounting for temporal autocorrelation.

GLMM Coefficient Plots

Comparison of GLMM model performance (AIC values) across species

3. Canonical Correspondence Analysis (CCA)

Purpose: Explore how community composition relates directly to environmental gradients.

Key Findings: CCA revealed the relationships between species distributions and environmental gradients. The biplot shows how different species are positioned along environmental axes, indicating their habitat preferences.

4. Joint Species Distribution Models (JSDMs)

Purpose: Model the occurrence of multiple species simultaneously, accounting for both shared environmental responses and species interactions.

Key Findings: JSDMs identified patterns of species co-occurrence and their shared responses to environmental variables. Species were clustered based on similar environmental responses.

5. Time Series and State-Space Models

Purpose: Investigate temporal dynamics and potential feedbacks in the system.

Key Findings: Time series analysis revealed seasonal patterns in both environmental variables and species abundance. SARIMAX models identified significant relationships between species abundance and environmental covariates over time.
